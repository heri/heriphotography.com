Starting as a pro photographer – guide

January 5, 2014 Business, Photography montreal 0 Comments

So you want to start out as a pro photographer? Making it a profession means it will pay your rent, mortgage, bills, as well as your business expenses, 365 days a year. As such, it’s not for everyone. It’s easy to take pictures of an event or snap a portrait of your cousin but being able to do it everyday, despite all odds, is not for everyone. You will loose opportunities to competition, to people who do the job for free, you’ll have to learn constantly, and be discouraged by customers who will turn towards user-generated media.

However, if you are willing to put in the effort and time, being a pro photographer can be hugely satisfying. You meet many creative people everyday, such as dancers, actors, make-up artists, models, activists, entrerpreneurs, powerful politicians or entrepreneurs who make headlines. You will have lots of fans raving about you and following your footsteps. You will have a Google-able name. You will travel often to be on place where there’s the action. You will hunt after sunsets, fog and the elusive light. You’ll be able to reveal and magnify the beauty, hideousness, vices of humankind and nature and perhaps contribute into making photography an art form.

Here are the main points to work on, if you are starting out as a pro photographer, with a few resources dedicated to Montreal, QC, Canada.

Canon EOS-M : Small camera, Big results

December 31, 2013 Photography, Video Gear, video production 1080p adapters canon timelapse 3 Comments

The Canon EOS-M gives you amazing pictures in the palm of your hand

The EOS-M is Canon’s first adventure in mirrorless land : a large sensor in a compact body. Right away, let me tell what the Canon is NOT : a replacement for your DSLR, an A camera, a portrait camera, or a camera you are going to win gigs with. The camera has the worst focus in Canon’s DSLR camera range, with focus hunting and also very small precision that makes it unfit for most action shots or serious jobs such as fashion or editorial. The Canon EOS-M excels in a few categories though : high-quality image in a compact, interchangeable body.

Tilta BMPCC cage and rig : a perfect match for your pocket camera

December 31, 2013 Video Gear blackmagic bmpcc cinema camera rig tilta 9 Comments

The Tilta cage is simply the best cage for the Pocket camera. It protects, gives it character, is affordable and lets you extend the BMPCC

The Blackmagic Pocket cinema camera (or BMPCC) shoots 1080p in raw or Prores, with a micro four thirds mount, all within a compact camera form that’s no bigger than a Panasonic GX7 or a Sony RX100.

Add a Panasonic pancake lens, and you’ve got in your pocket a cinema camera with a codec better than a Canon C100, a Panasonic GH3, a Digital Bolex, or a $7000 Sony FS700

Why do you need a rig for the BMPCC?

For serious projects, here are a few problems :

  • the screen scratches easily and it’s is very hard to nail focus
  • there’s a grip but it’s small. The camera in itself is small as well, so it’s  difficult to get stable shots
  • no cold shoe or accessory points. How do you add power, monitors, or an audio recorder to the BMPCC?
  • it has a micro HDMI out to plug a monitor but the it’s very easy to unplug a micro HDMI cable
  • audio pre-amps are weak
  • the sensor is very small so your usual wide angle lens becomes a telephoto lens. Also you need to get the sharpest lenses since the camera will work on the center only

For the last point, you can get a SpeedBooster. For the rest, this is where a Tilta rig comes: it will protect your cinema camera, add mounting points to add other accessories such as follow focuses, monitors etc., make it production ready with a HDMI out that doesn’t move, and much more.

Olympus 45mm f1.8 for video – Review

December 26, 2013 Video Gear Olympus 45mm f1.8 Panasonic 20mm f1.7 pancake Panasonic GH3 1 Comment

The Olympus 45mm f1.8 is a portrait lens that is ideally matched with an Olympus body such as OMD-EM1, OMD-EM5, with their 5 stops image stabilized sensor and “popping” colors from Olympus.

I use mainly the Panasonic 20mm as an all-around lens for the Panasonic GH3 – the focal length is flexible and great for semi-wide shots, interviews, document events and more.

The perspective from the Panasonic 20mm pancake is boring though, and I have found the Olympus 45mm f1.8 complements very well the 20mm. With an equivalent focal length of 20mm and a very fast auto-focus, you can do a quick close-up on a person’s face, an object in the scenery.

Timeless Portraits

July 1, 2013 heriportrait 0 Comments

“Timeless portraits” is Heri’s personal project, with the goal of creating timeless portraits, each showing a special character, and each inviting the viewer to have a second look, because it’s mysterious, mesmerizing, beautiful. Is it the eyes? Is it the pose? Is the character behind the expression? Who is the person photographed? What’s that little detail she’s wearing or in the background? It’s hard to answer. Think Mona Lisa, the girl with the Pearl Earring, or the Afghan Girl‘s eyes.

The photos will be published later this year, between 7 to 12 portraits. Follow this blog for updates, or check out a preview on 500px or on instagram.
