Top 11 websites for filmmakers

December 27, 2013 video production cinematography filmmaking video production 0 Comments

Looking to take your filmmaking and video production skills up a notch? The following 11 sites will help step up your game in business, production, editing and post-production.

  • NoFilmSchool a site for DIY filmmakers and independent creatives, with a good mix of gear news, creative advice, and also has a solid community of filmmakers commenting on each post.It offers solutions to how to get the most out of the things you create in order to sustain a long career as a filmmaker, writer, director, producer, editor, cinematographers and much more.
  • Youtube is the world’s most popular video popular and the 2nd biggest search engine. Their servers are good, switching easily between different resolutions and also works well on iOS, Android and most mobile devices. The video transcoding is not the best, but you can upload in 4K, as well as 1440p. It also has great channels such as FilmRiot, DigitalRev, B&H excellent workshop series, TheCameraStoreTV, Check out Heri Photography there.
  • Filmmaker IQ is run by a group of filmmakers who discuss a range of topics, such as as make-Up, Camera comparisons, audio, cinematography and more. They answer both the whys and hows of filmmaking and help us understand the new media wave, without forgetting the old.

  • With almost a billion users, Facebook is a must for filmmakers. For discussion groups open only for filmmakers to discuss the arts & Business, to interacting with actors and teams, Facebook lets you know what’s coming up. Here’s our page.
  • Vimeo has one of the video transcoding – it’s almost ProRes quality, with the colors, sharpness almost similar to the one you’ve uploaded. They also have a video school, a great music library, as well as monetization options for filmmakers. Check out HeriPhotography there.
  • ModelMayhem is a directory of models, make-up artists, and photographers. You can use it to find models & extras for fashion and editorial photoshoots, but also for music videos and experimental cinematography. Here’s heriphotography on that page
  • You can find new ideas and see what the market is looking for on crowdfunding websites Indiegogo and Kickstarter. Indiegogo was put up together initially to support new indie film productions, but Kickstarter has lots of success stories as well. Indiegogo also has a distribution company called Distribber, to get your short on netflix, itunes and viewers
  • CinemaSquid is a library of looks, whenever you’re looking for that special lighting, set design or actor interaction. It also help the team visualize what they’re looking after.
  • EOSHD is a blog by Andrew Reid, british cinematographer focused on the best technical camera equipment for indie filmmakers. There’s no compromise and the Canikon companies get bashed regularly for not innovating fast enough. He’s often on the bleeding edge to get the best image acquisition camera from a limited budget.
  • FStoppers has a mix of posts on photography and videography, with good posts on lighting, new camera equipment, make-up ideas, new creative photography, and as such, is a great blog for cinematographers.
  • U, S and Toby is an excellent resource for filmmakers, with lots of advice and resources, whenever you are stuck on something specific (production, audio, creative, script etc.). Here’s a specific list that we like.
