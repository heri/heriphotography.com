Tilta BMPCC cage and rig : a perfect match for your pocket camera

December 31, 2013 Video Gear blackmagic bmpcc cinema camera rig tilta 9 Comments

The Tilta cage is simply the best cage for the Pocket camera. It protects, gives it character, is affordable and lets you extend the BMPCC

The Blackmagic Pocket cinema camera (or BMPCC) shoots 1080p in raw or Prores, with a micro four thirds mount, all within a compact camera form that’s no bigger than a Panasonic GX7 or a Sony RX100.

Add a Panasonic pancake lens, and you’ve got in your pocket a cinema camera with a codec better than a Canon C100, a Panasonic GH3, a Digital Bolex, or a $7000 Sony FS700

Why do you need a rig for the BMPCC?

For serious projects, here are a few problems :

  • the screen scratches easily and it’s is very hard to nail focus
  • there’s a grip but it’s small. The camera in itself is small as well, so it’s  difficult to get stable shots
  • no cold shoe or accessory points. How do you add power, monitors, or an audio recorder to the BMPCC?
  • it has a micro HDMI out to plug a monitor but the it’s very easy to unplug a micro HDMI cable
  • audio pre-amps are weak
  • the sensor is very small so your usual wide angle lens becomes a telephoto lens. Also you need to get the sharpest lenses since the camera will work on the center only

For the last point, you can get a SpeedBooster. For the rest, this is where a Tilta rig comes: it will protect your cinema camera, add mounting points to add other accessories such as follow focuses, monitors etc., make it production ready with a HDMI out that doesn’t move, and much more.

Tilta is a Chinese company that offers high-quality rigs at affordable prices for the latest generation of cameras.  You can order the rig on eBay ($327 for the lowest price here) or through third-party distributors such as Ikan, and once payement is processed, it takes less than a week to be shipped & delivered.

Specs

This is what you will get:

  • 1 x TiLTA BMPC Cage,with a wooden grip and aluminium body, designed for the camera’s unique form factor , with 1/4″ accessory mount points
  • 1 x Top Handle with 2 cold shoes that matches the cage
  • 1 x 15mm Rod Clamp with Quick Release baseplate.
  • 2 x 300mm matching Aluminium rods

Unboxing


The unit comes from Shenzen, China through DHL, bubble-wrapped.



Clockwise : HMDI port protector, top handle, cage armor + screwdriver, baseplate. There’s as well a minimalist instructions sheet.



One major con of the cage is that it doesn’t sit by itself (doh!). So that means from now on, it’ll lean akwardly.



This is where you notice the quality of the materials. It’s not a cheap “chineese” product! Notice as well the engraved logos



The BMPCC with the Tilta cage and Sigma 18-55mm f1.8 zoom lens. Notice the HMDI plug protector

Review

From the first look, the rig screams quality and it’s easy to compare it to an Apple product — minus the premium price.

Mounting the cage takes less than 5 minutes and screwdrivers and other tools are provided in the box. The cage leaves space around the sides and bottom of camera so you can swap easily the SD card or batteries, and also add easily audio cables and other cables without having to remove the cage while working.

The Tilta cage is well-designed, easy to use and delivers more than promised

When you’re finishing mounting the cage, everything feels solid, secure and adjustable — just what you need to work on a professional production.

The handle matches well the cage and has 2 cold shoes. It can be slided at your convenience. It allows you to transport securily the unit without fearing something would break in the process.

All in all, the Tilta cage is very impressive for the asking price of $327: very well-designed, easy-to-use, with high-quality materials, and delivers more than promised.

Conclusion

Pros

  • Best cage for the BMPCC
  • Great build quality, precision-machining with many mounting points to extend rig
  • Great value for the money
  • A rig ready for production — with all the mounting points you need
  • Fast shipping
  • Unique features such as micro HDMI port protector and a micro HDMI to HDMI cable not even mentioned in the product description

Cons:

  • The cage or the quick release plate doesn’t sit by itself
  • Weight distribution is towards the right

Rating : 4.5/5 Highly recommended for Blackmagic Pocket Cinema Camera owners.

At this point, all this needs is a follow focus as well as a good monitor and you’re in business!
