Foulab : Montreal’s creative space for hardware makers

January 29, 2014 Photography Canon EOS-M montreal Photo report 0 Comments

Foulab is a space for hardware makers, with the idea that any smart individual can imagine and create new innovative electronics.

Foulab is at the intersection of geekiness, art, hacking, and maker movement.

A few metro stations away from downtown Montreal, Foulab is located in an industrial building, the kind you would expect to see hosting a rave party.

Before coming to Foulab, you’ll see a studio for artists:

Fiercely independent, Foulab is not sponsored by any government or corporation. It’s run by its members, in a way similar to coops. Members join in to work on various maker projects, learn electronics or meet other like-minded montrealers:

Sigma 18-35mm F1.8 Art Lens : a lens for beautiful pictures

January 4, 2014 Photography, Video Gear canon Canon EOS-M Sigma 0 Comments

The Sigma 18-35mm is like an Audi : amazing engineering, understatement and impressive performance

First impressions of the Sigma

In matte black, with solid weight and high-quality materials, the new Sigma 18-35mm is the only zoom in the world with an F1.8 aperture. It combines quality, affordability and versatility in one single lens.

It invites you to take it out to take portraits, street photography, go to events, and take beautiful pictures. With only one switch for manual/autofocus, it doesn’t get in the way.

I am using currently the Sigma 18-35mm art lens with a Canon EOS-M (Read Canon EOS-M review). Its F1.8 aperture is great for evening pictures, or portraits with great bokeh.
