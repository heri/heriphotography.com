Sigma 18-35mm F1.8 Art Lens : a lens for beautiful pictures

January 4, 2014 Photography, Video Gear canon Canon EOS-M Sigma 0 Comments

The Sigma 18-35mm is like an Audi : amazing engineering, understatement and impressive performance

First impressions of the Sigma

In matte black, with solid weight and high-quality materials, the new Sigma 18-35mm is the only zoom in the world with an F1.8 aperture. It combines quality, affordability and versatility in one single lens.

It invites you to take it out to take portraits, street photography, go to events, and take beautiful pictures. With only one switch for manual/autofocus, it doesn’t get in the way.

I am using currently the Sigma 18-35mm art lens with a Canon EOS-M (Read Canon EOS-M review). Its F1.8 aperture is great for evening pictures, or portraits with great bokeh.

WHAT I LOVE ABOUT THE SIGMA 18-35mm

SHARPNESS – Photo labs (dxomark, photozone) demonstrate that the Sigma is the sharpest zoom for APS-C cameras, if not the sharpest. It also has low chromatic aberration, low vignetting (that can be entirely corrected in Lightroom/Aperture), and good color rendition.

DESIGN – The build screams professional, akin to the Canon L lenses. The focusing and zoom mechanisms have no play and are smooth. Overall, design is beautiful and minimalist. Previously, Sigma had cheap-looking gold markings on its lenses as well as cheap materials for the barrel that would chaff after a few months. Now the design takes lessons from Zeiss lenses design and Canon Ls. Good move by Sigma!

SMOOTH HANDLING – Focusing is fast, as fast as your body can be. I find it’s faster than many other prime lenses I own. Simply, it doesn’t get in the way of getting amazing pictures.

PRICE – You can find the Nikon or Canon mount of this lens for $799 – a very reasonable price for a price. This is like getting a Porsche 911 for the price of a Toyota. A great move by Sigma

PERFECT FOR VIDEO – If cover a live event, having this lens is like having 3 primes at the same time : a wide, a semi-wide and a normal. Having the Sigma 18-35mm means not missing a shot. Instead of unmounting and mounting a wider or longer lens, you can reframe the shot in a pinch. For video, the f1.8 aperture means you can shoot in dark conference rooms or at music shows without increasing too much ISOs so the overall image won’t be grainy. In addition, it will show excellent separation between your subject and the environment, so your images will stand out from the competition.

Note : For m43s or Blackmagic shooter, please beware that you have to get the Nikon version of this lens! There’s no Speedbooster yet for the Canon mount.

MONTREAL SIGHT-SEEING

I’ve been a montrealer for the past years.

Montreal is a city full of surprises and diversity.

The winter is a great moment for exploration and discovery in Montreal. Wake up at early hours of the mornings and you will see breathtaking ice & snow landscapes. It’s humbling and makes you think of the past french trappeurs of the past.

Temperatures drop down to -40C (-25C and -40C with the windchill). That means you get cold bite if you are exposing your hands. The Sigma 18-35mm is perfect for those photographic journeys, since you don’t have to change lenses and not expose your hands.

Here’s a shot of the St-Laurent River, Montreal, taken with the Sigma 18-35mm:

Not everyone knows it, but Montréal is an island, with the river St-Laurent around it. In the winter, ice, fog and nature’s elements flow around the island. We walked and stopped often for minutes to fully appreciate the sunrise.

Using the Sigma 18-35mm is simple: zoom to desired focal length, compose, snap.

I shoot often wide open at any focal lengths – or get down all the way to f8 and more if shooting landscape. A small downside is the 35mm limit – you wish quickly for a 50mm or 85mm focal length.

The Sigma invites you to go outdoors or walk around and take those beautiful pictures.

WHAT I DON’T LOVE ABOUT THE SIGMA 18-35mm

35mm LIMIT – I wish it goes all the way up to 50mm to make it a great portrait lens.

NO IS – Image stabilization would be handy for videography. Hopefully the F1.8 aperture helps a lot.

RARE AVAILABILITY – Because of its performance, it’s hard to find the Sigma. Get it if you find it in a store!

Conclusion

In summary, the Sigma 18-35mm:

  • gives you sharp images wide open, at all focal lengths
  • produces excellent photos for event, astrophotography, nighttime, portrait photography with its F1.8 aperture, a world record
  • is sharper than many wide and normal lenses – you don’t have to switch lenses anymore

It should come as no surprise that I love this lens!

Rating: 5/5 Highly recommended for photography and videography.
